Installment vs. Upfront

At 0% installment your total outlay is identical either way — so the only real difference is the return you earn on cash you haven't paid out yet. This models that float against a liquid-asset yield.

How it reads: you set aside the full upfront amount today and invest it. Each period you withdraw one installment to pay the plan; the balance left in the account keeps earning. The closing invested balance after the final payment is your net gain — pure interest when the plan is genuinely 0%.

Returns aren't guaranteed and this ignores tax on investment income, any liquidity lock-in, and the discipline cost of not spending the parked cash. It's a decision aid, not advice.
