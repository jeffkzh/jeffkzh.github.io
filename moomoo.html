How to maximise it

  • Deposit exactly to your chosen tier's threshold, not beyond. Rewards are fixed per tier, so excess capital only dilutes your return. For most people the lowest tier is the best percentage play; step up a tier only if you'd genuinely invest that capital anyway.
  • Complete all deposits within 30 days of your first deposit. Later top-ups don't count toward higher tiers — that's a hard rule in the T&C. And it must be cash from a bank; transferring shares in from another broker doesn't count toward any tier.
  • Activate the 6% p.a. Cash Plus booster. Confirmed in this campaign's pack: your first Cash Plus subscription (single subscription of RM500+) earns a subsidised 6% p.a. for 30 days, on up to RM30,000. Park the deposit there from day one; after the 30 days it reverts to the fund's base yield (~3.5% p.a.).
  • Don't let net deposit fall below your tier. Withdrawals subtract from your cumulative net deposit; dropping below the threshold before the lock completes forfeits the reward.
  • Claim, don't just deposit. Moomoo requires you to click "Claim" on the campaign page after hitting the threshold — the 60-day lock and reward issuance flow from there.
  • Sell the Apple shares promptly if you don't want US exposure. The shares are granted at a fixed RM value, but from crediting onward they move with Apple's price and USD/MYR. Selling soon after crediting locks in the value, minus a small FX/spread cost.

The zero-market-risk route (almost)

You can qualify without buying a single stock: deposit to your tier, leave the cash in Cash Plus for the 60 days, claim, receive the shares, and sell them once credited. Two honest caveats make this "low risk," not "no risk":

  • The reward itself is stock, not cash. Between crediting and your sale, the value rides Apple's share price and the USD/MYR rate. It's usually days, but it's real exposure — unlike a cash reward.
  • The 20% rule. If you do invest the deposit and your holdings fall more than 20%, the fund-locking task can fail (Moomoo says to contact support for reissuance). Keeping the deposit in Cash Plus avoids ever tripping this.
  • Cash Plus is a money-market fund — low risk, but not a bank deposit, not PIDM-insured, and the yield floats.

Using the coupons

  • The 2× RM25 stock cash coupons activate immediately when you claim them and are valid for 90 days. Execute one eligible trade per coupon within that window and the coupon value is rebated in cash within 10 trading days (it doesn't offset the order amount). Use a liquid, low-cost trade to trigger them.
  • The RM50 cash coupon needs no trade, but it unlocks only after you've maintained the RM1,000+ net deposit for the full 60 days — then credits within 10 trading days.

The risks and fine print

  • Capital is committed for 60 days — plus the 30-day deposit window means the full cycle can run ~3 months. That's liquidity and opportunity cost.
  • Reward value isn't guaranteed in ringgit terms once shares are credited — Apple's price and FX move it until you sell. Moomoo explicitly takes no responsibility for fluctuations between purchase and crediting.
  • Apple share rewards are capped at 10,000 units campaign-wide, first come first served — if they run out before you complete the task, you don't get them.
  • Rewards aren't instant: after the 60-day lock completes, issuance takes up to 10 trading days, and you must have clicked "Claim" on the campaign page first.
  • Strict single-account rules. One account per person and per device; anything that looks like multi-accounting can void rewards entirely.
  • Thresholds and terms shift between campaign seasons — Moomoo runs overlapping promos (store events, Cash Plus boosters, carnival draws) with different eligibility cut-off dates. Read the specific campaign you're registered under.
  • The reassuring part: Moomoo Securities Malaysia is licensed by the Securities Commission Malaysia (eCMSL/A0397/2024), and the deposit itself stays your money throughout — only the reward is conditional.
